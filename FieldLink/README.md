# FieldLink 

Data and telephony connectivity in a briefcase

## Vision 

FieldLink is, literally, a briefcase (node) containing everything needed to rapidly get a location online with data and/or telephone in a very easy, non-technical deployment. 

A variety of network connectivity will be automatically discovered - from wired Internet, WiFi client, cellular (LTE/5G), and AREDN.

AREDN will be the primary driver of network connectivity. Each node will attempt to connect to tunnels over the Internet (if available), and provides PoE "Device-to-Device" ports for RF and node-to-node connectivity.

Each node provides WiFi access for devices to connect to, and at least 6 PoE ports for LAN connectivity (including phones). Other node-specific options may include analog phone jacks (supporting fax machines as well as telephones) and phone interface ports (Ex: to plug into an EoC network).

## Nodes 

### FieldLink 1 - Naddabum 
Provides 4 PoE D2D ports, 10 PoE access ports (for phones, computers, etc), 8 analog phone line ports. Target use is to provide connectivity and demos at Hamfests.

### FieldLink 2 - Quartermaster 
Provides 4 PoE D2D ports, 6 PoE access ports, 4 analog phone line interfaces ("FXO", to connect to other phone networks/landlines such as at an EoC), and 2 analog phone line ports.
